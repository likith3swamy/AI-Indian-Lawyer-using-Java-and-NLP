Your Question

What is the scope of Article 14 of the Indian Constitution and how has the judiciary interpreted "equality before the law"?

AIL Answer

Summary:

Article 14 guarantees equality before the law and equal protection of the laws. The judiciary has interpreted Article 14 to prohibit class legislation and to require reasonable classification: (1) classification must be based on an intelligible differentia, and (2) differentia must have a rational relation to the object sought to be achieved.
